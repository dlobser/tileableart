Syllabus

This course explores possible visions of the future where Bits, Atoms, and the human are seamlessly coupled to create a shared, tangible augmented reality, or "Future Reality", that people can share using all their senses. We review (1) existing visions, (2) techniques to hack human senses, (3) enabling VR/AR/TUI technologies, (4) new application scenarios (e.g. collaborative design, learning), and (5) social & cultural implications of future reality.

This is a project based course wherein individuals will complete two assignments in a 7 week period. The first assignment will be to present a proposal or a pitch for the final project. Your idea can be hopeful, cautionary, educational, poignant or comical. The key goal is to illuminate, in a thought provoking way, societal issues around the possibilities created by future shared augmented and malleable realities. The second project will be a more fully fleshed out presentation in a medium of your choice. This can include power point presentations, comic strips, videos, VR experiences, interactive stories etc.

Homework #1

Share one project idea and answer the question "why are you in this class?" - brainstorm 12 ideas total and then cull.

1: extrapolate current technologies a little bit but otherwise assume the world is mostly the same as it is now

  • scrubbing home movies (black mirror)
  • facial recognition
  • advertising
  • quantified self
  • gaming
  • hacking
  • aesthetic augmentation (fasion/architecture)

2: imagine a sci fi world where something besides 'future reality' has had a profound impact on the world

  • global warming
  • bionic limbs
  • artificial intelligence
  • killer robots
  • mind control / mind hacking
  • convincing, completely simulated realities

3: dare to be absurd, use exquisite corpse, record your dreams

  • individualized, mood based facial hair
  • pheremone sensor with visual feedback
  • streaming internal monologue actualizer
  • everyone else has your face and talks in your voice
  • mental illness simulator

The schedule, roughly

  • Week 1: Intros / Read: As We May Think / Write down 3 initial ideas / Buy: New Media Reader
  • Week 2: 3 ideas presentation / history of mixed reality / how past tech has affected human communication
  • Week 3: non verbal communication (gestures, sign language, unsconscious communication, body language) / chalktalk
  • Week 4: mid term presentations
  • Week 5: security / privacy / hacking / surveillance / utopia,dystopia / digital divide
  • Week 6: discuss finals / technical issues with developing mixed reality technology
  • Week 7: final presentations
